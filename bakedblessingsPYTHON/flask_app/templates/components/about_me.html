About Me

My favorite way to show love is to serve, and my favorite way to serve is to feed the people I love.

Growing up in Indonesia, I didn't spend a lot of time in the kitchen because we had a helper who did the cooking, but as I got older I loved cooking and baking for special occasions and it became something I looked forward to. It wasn't until I was an adult that I realized how much people appreciate having someone else cook or bake for them. Spending time in the kitchen is often thought of as time consuming and can be exhausting, or just simply something people don't want to do because there are so many other things to do.

Since leaving Indonesia and moving to the US in March 2020, I have had the opportunity to stay home to homeschool our two boys. Although I take jobs outside the home here and there, I get to focus on our home life more than ever before. A lot of home life includes feeding my family and this kindled my desire to expand my ability to cook from scratch using more traditional methods. Often, using more traditional methods produce higher quality products. That is what I have found with making my own sourdough bread with a homegrown starter. I have no desire to buy bread made in a factory ever again!

Someday, my husband and I hope to live off the land more or less, provide for ourselves and our neighbors (who will include our family), and who knows, maybe trade my baked goods for eggs and milk straight from the farmer next door! In the meantime, I sell my goods to those in our community who are too busy with the responsibilities of life to spend hours in their kitchen. I am delighted to fill that role of the kitchen dweller to provide my community with baked blessings!
